Introduction

FPW was developed by Wall Street Quantitative Systems Inc (WSQSI).  The PC based FPW program is about 145 MB. With the basic database it will be around 15 GB and it can go beyond several hundred GB if a full database is loaded.  Even now, many PC can not handle it.  It's this reason that slowed FPW's commercialized steps.  Now we are using the newest net technologies to make it available via the Web in order to reach as many as possible users.  The first full version of the trading software and testing platform was finished in 1994 and has only been used in-house since. After 10 years of development and refining, this software is complete and powerful now. FPW developers are all real frontline traders or fund managers.  Therefore it has been uniquely developed from the traders prospective, only considering what traders need to make their decisions.

Think about the advantages of FPW: With a couple of lines of coding and a couple of mouse clicks one can simulate almost any pattern one can imagine with fully managing rules of stops, exits and objectives, scaling in or out, over one or a group of markets with same or different managing rules for each market. And all of this can be done via the Web; one can reach it any time from anywhere.  After receiving the simulation results, the software will further automatically find the premature conditions to improve the model’s quality and analyze it in relation to your other trading models to see how it affects your whole trading system.  This is what Financial PatternWorkshop is designed to do and is what it has been doing successfully for us.  Now, it is ready to work for you.  Frankly, even many big trading houses do not have this ability yet.  Users do not need to spend hundreds of thousands of dollars to equip a high end computing system and to hire experts to maintain the very expensive financial database.  Even after you have the hardware, there is no other software like FPW available.

Let’s consider several simple examples. Example one: Between time A (let’s say 10:10am) and time B (say 2:05pm), when the market (say SP index futures) first hits Price C (say just a fixed level) and triggers a signal if it later hits Price D before time B (say a fixed value plus  the low between time A and the current or price C plus a fixed value E) and hold it for X time (say days or hours) with certain stops, exits and objectives according to the market conditions.  PW does this in just 1 line.  Example two: Between time A and time B (in days or just minutes like the first example) when one market (say Crude Oil Future) hits price C (say a new high) to trigger a signal of another market (say to buy Fuel Oil Future) and hold for X time (say 2 day and 2 hours or just 10 minutes) with certain stops, exits and objectives according to the market conditions.  PW does this in just 1 line, too.  Example three: It takes one click to get NYSE most active, percentage/net change or any criteria one sets,  during any period (say the last month or week, the last hours or minutes, not only from the open of the month, week or day). 

FPW is divided into two independent parts: PatternWorkshop (PW) and MarketPulse (MP).  MP has special tools for those whose research area is about stock markets.  FPW is designed to be simple to use.  It takes less than one hour for one to begin his or her first PW simulation and requires no more than 10 hours to learn all the tools.  In a week or two, one can become a PW expert.  As for MP, any one can learn to use it in minutes. 

Note: Tick data means intraday data or intraday bars (5 minutes bars in most cases) except specially explained.

Before using FPW, certain precautions should be kept in mind.  While developing a system, one should always remember that there is no way one can maximize one’s profit and minimize one’s risk at the same time.  In order to design systems with the highest probability of working in the future, blind tests and add noise tests should be done on all systems.  Each system should also be limited to a few parameters and rules to reduce the likelihood that the results are curve fitted.  Modeling is a science, yet trading is an art.  It’s really not easy to combine these two together in the real world.  All problems are related to chasing perfection.  Anything perfect is not real.

Finally, we hope everyone has fun using FPW and finds profitable models to make money!
